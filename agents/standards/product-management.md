# Product Management Standards

## PRD Structure
Every Product Requirement Document must include:
1. **Executive Summary**: Vision and high-level solution.
2. **Strategic Context**: Problem statement and user impact.
3. **Success Metrics**: e.g., Conversion, Retention, Task Completion.
4. **User Journeys**: Key paths through the feature.
5. **Functional Requirements**: Actionable, numbered list.
6. **Non-Functional Requirements**: Technical constraints (security, performance).
7. **Out of Scope**: Explicitly defined boundaries.

## User Stories
Every story must meet the "Definition of Ready":
- **Statement**: "As a [type of user], I want to [action], so that [value/benefit]."
- **Acceptance Criteria**: Comprehensive list of conditions, including edge cases and error states.
- **Technical Notes**: Constraints or considerations for implementation.

## Prioritization
- Use RICE (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) or similar structured frameworks.
- Focus on the North Star metric and MVP. Cut "low impact / high effort" features.
- Fall in love with the problem, not the solution.

## Engineering Collaboration
- **Authoritative decisions:** Resolve ambiguities with clear, direct calls — not "suggestions."
- **Single source of truth:** Update primary documentation immediately when decisions are made.
- **Trade-off transparency:** Explain the rationale, especially when cutting features for speed.
- **Outcome over output:** Moving the metric matters more than shipping the feature.
